<p><h2>
Seven Wonders of the Ancient World is the first known list of the most remarkable man-made creations of classical antiquity, and was based on guide-books popular among Hellenic sight-seers and only includes works located around the Mediterranean rim. The number seven was chosen because the Greeks believed it to be the representation of perfection and plenty.
The seven wonders of the world first came approximate 2200 years ago. In the ancient world, the idea of seven wonders came to Herodotus and Callimachus.
